Learning Paths in Mathematics

Derived Algebraic Geometry

A structured learning path from fundamentals to advanced topics.

Prerequisites

Basic background in scheme theory and algebraic topology required.

1. Category Theory Foundations

  • Read first two chapters of Schapira's lecture notes
  • Study Gabriel-Zisman's "Calculus of fractions and homotopy theory" (Ch. I and II)

2. Abstract Homotopy Theory

  • Cisinski's notes covering simplicial sets and model categories
  • Quillen's "Homotopical algebra" (Chapter I)
  • Cisinski's mini-course notes on stable ∞-categories
  • Joyal's notes on quasi-categories

3. Derived Commutative Algebra

  • Moerdijk-Toen: "Simplicial Methods for Operads and Algebraic Geometry" (Lecture 4, Part II)
  • Lurie's thesis (Section 3)
  • Toen-Vezzosi's HAG II (Section 2.2.2)

4. Stacks and Advanced Topics

  • Toen's notes on algebraic stacks
  • Preygel's notes
  • Lurie's "On ∞-topoi"

5. Recommended Papers for Practice

  • Antieau-Gepner: "Brauer groups and étale cohomology in derived algebraic geometry"
  • Bhatt: "p-adic derived de Rham cohomology"
  • Toen: "Derived Azumaya algebras"
  • Gaitsgory-Rozenblyum: "A study in derived algebraic geometry"
